I enjoyed making my free motion quilted log cabin quilt and wanted another quilt to practise more FMQ on! I chose to make large “right and left” blocks as this block as a big square of empty space in the middle which would be perfect for practising. For the centre square of the blocks, I chose a plain fabric so that my quilting would stand out more.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I made some green, blue and pick blocks and started to lay them out to see how they would go together. This quilt would be a “quilt as you go” quilt to use up some of my wadding scraps and also to see if I liked the idea of “quilt as you go”. The benefit of quilt as you go is that you can quilt on much smaller sections of the quilt rather than the whole quilt, which can be good, especially if your machine has a small throat. However, the downside is you then have to sew together the finished bits which as well as being a faff on with all the layers you then have to hem the back seam. I decided that quilt as you go wasn’t really for me, but at least I tried it.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
