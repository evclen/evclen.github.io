This quilt was my first attempt at a scenic picture quilt, one day I hope to make a full size one. This one is just a wall hanging measuring about 80cm square, it really looks great on our wall!<\/p>\n\n\n\n

To create this quilt, I drew the mountain out onto a grid of 1 inch squares half the size I wanted. I thought that basing the quilt off 2 inch squares would be manageable and would turn out around the size that I wanted. After drawing the mountain, I then adjusted the mountain to as much as possible consist of squares or half square triangles, this would make it much easier to piece. I did end up with some pieces consisting of half rectangle triangles and other irregular squares, but it wasn\u2019t too many, so I just went with it. I used flying geese to depict the trees and 4 inch squares for the sky. I laid out the fabrics on the floor before sewing together so I could get the right colour arrangement before piecing.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

For the quilting I did some tree shaped lines for the trees and grassy tufts for the grass hill, some close ups are shown below. The sky I did wobbly lines with a walking foot and the mountain free motion scribbles going the same way that the rock went.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
