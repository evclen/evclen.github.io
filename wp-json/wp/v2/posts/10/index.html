A colourful single bed sized quilt made from 2.5 inch square scraps. Even though the design was simple I could never get board working with fabrics that are so bright and varied, it was a joy to make.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

My Mum has a box of scraps cut into 2.5\u201d squares and sorted by colour. I thought a checkerboard design would add some consistency to the vast quantity of random colours so together we matched the squares into pairs of fabric that would complement each other. Choosing fabrics that go together is one of my favourite things about quilting and with scraps it is even more exciting. Every piece has a story as all the fabrics have been pre-used. While matching them up my mum and I reminisced about old quilts that she had made with the fabrics, items of clothes that they had come from or the specific fabric shop that we had bought the fabric from. of choice when matching up the fabrics, they may not go well together but with scraps there may not be other options and I like been forced to use colours that I would not usually put together, it is very exciting.
