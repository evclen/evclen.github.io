I made a quilt years ago for my friend Gabby\u2019s 18th<\/sup> birthday. It was a Japanese-inspired quilt made of fabric panels of geisha and large blocks made from Japanese fabric. She loves it, but it is an odd shape and does not fit the double bed she now has. I offered to make her and her fianc\u00e9e a new one and hoped to have it ready in time for a wedding present. I hoped to combine all the quilting skills I have developed to make a masterpiece.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Gabby and I made a joint Pinterest board, this was so that I could see what sort of quilt she might like. The general ideas that I took from the board was a that Gabby wanted a light coloured, modern quilt with reasonably plain fabrics and not too many different colours. A few examples of quilts on the board are these 3 from Suzy Quilts, the thrive quilt https:\/\/suzyquilts.com\/shop\/thrive-quilt-pattern-download\/<\/a>, the new horizons quilt https:\/\/suzyquilts.com\/shop\/new-horizons-quilt-pattern-download\/<\/a> and an ombre quilt https:\/\/suzyquilts.com\/create-an-ombre-quilt-with-fat-quarters\/<\/a>. I would say that I was more of a traditional quilter so looked forward to the challenge of trying a more modern quilt.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

I would have liked to take Gabby to the fabric shop while she was up visiting, but we just didn\u2019t have time, so I got her to pick some fabrics from my stash. I would then use these as inspiration for the quilt and later go to the shop by myself to get more fabric that would match. She came up with this combination of fabrics.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
